Tab management is one of those small frustrations that compounds over a working day. Open a few tabs for research, switch between tools, and before long you have dozens of unorganized windows slowing down your thinking.

Takt addresses this by letting you define rules that automatically group tabs by domain, keyword, or URL pattern. Rules run entirely in your browser using only locally stored data, with no external service required for the matching process.

In this post we explore how to structure your rules for common workflows and how to use tab groups effectively to keep your workspace clear.

Structuring your rules

A well-structured rule set mirrors how you actually work. If you spend time in a project management tool each morning and switch to documentation in the afternoon, two rules — one per domain — give you automatic group separation with no ongoing maintenance.

Start with the tabs you open most often. One rule per domain is usually enough.

Using color effectively

Color is the fastest visual signal your brain processes. Assigning distinct colors to groups you switch between often — development, documentation, communication — lets you locate a group at a glance rather than scanning tab titles.

The eight available colors map well to the major work buckets most people use. Takt applies the color automatically whenever a new tab matches a rule, so your workspace stays organized without any manual steps.
